Start-Up Guide

Prepare the RBMK-1500 reactor for a safe and stable startup by completing the Pre-Startup Checklist and configuring all supporting systems.


Stage One Startup


  • Grab a bypass key from the key cabinet at the door of the Control Room.
  • Insert the key into the Auto-scram panel and disable Auto-scram. (please note that this is not necceary, but highly encouraged as it it extremly annoying to a majority of players)
  • Enable Reactor Mode 1.
  • Close Heat Exchanger A and B.
  • Disable OCC Circulation Pumps 1 and 2. their by the electrical panel
  • Mode Conflict alarm will flash, press the acknowledge button on the Middle Reactor Console.
  • Enable Shutdown Control Rods. (Note for new players: the term "shutdown" is a bit of a misnomer, in this case it means to remove the shutdown rods)
  • Click the Reset button next to the acknowledge button on the Middle Reactor Console.
  • Wait for the A3 Rods Out of Zone panel to flash (Light B2), a bell will ring signalling the Shutdown Rods are fully removed.
  • Enable Auto Control Rods.

  • Type the numbers 1500 into the keypad next to the Auto Control Rods switch and click the grey button to set, the red button is to reset.

  • You also need to remove manual rods in a symetrical patten, we are currently working on a page with rod distribution patterns.
  • Once the reactor reaches 1500 MW enable Reactor Mode 2. Keep an eye on the Core Heat Distribution.
  • Wait until water temp is 200°-265
  • Enable Feedwater Pump 1 and set it to speed 2.
  • Enable Condensate Pump 1 and set it to speed 2.

Stage Two Startup

Withdraw manual Control Rods until power reaches 2100 MW, also set the auto rod controll to 2100MW, as it will insert the auto rods to 100% if you do not.

Turbine A Run-up


  • Call Engineering (the leftmost phone, dial 106), click Turning gear, and disengage Turbine A.
  • At the Turbine Panel enable the Synchroscope for Turbine A, it is on the wall
    • if the syncroscope does not spin use the bypass key and make sure both breakers are enabed.<>
  • Turn on the auto bypass and set anywhere from 20-50MW.
  • Slowly increase the Turbine Valve to around 60-70%
  • The RPM should start rising, if it doesnt you are not making steam; check your reactor MW.
  • Open the Grid Sync cap.
  • Once the RPM reaches around 1770-1830 RPM the Synchroscope will flash and make an audible sound when the needle passes over the zone at the top of the gauge.
  • Once the needle slows down and the RPM is around 1800, wait for the green light to light up and then click Grid Sync.
  • If you synced it correctly the RPM will stabilize at 1800.

If sync is unsuccessful


  • Click the Acknowledge button, the corresponding one to silence the Turbine Trip Alarm will be glowing.
  • Wait for the RPM to drop below 600.
  • Click the reset button next to the Acknowledge button you just pressed.
  • Retry the Turbine Sync Process.

If sync is successful


  • Acknowledge the Reverse Power alert.
  • Increase the Turbine Valve SLOWLY to 100%, preferibly with the precise valve
  • Decrease the Bypass valve to 0% or near 0%.
  • Once the generator load is positive click the Reset button and Acknowledge button.
  • Enable Breaker 64 A-M, it is on the wall.
  • Call Engineering and click Reset Breakers.
  • Set both Deaerator A and B Relief Valves to 25%.
  • Set both Deaerator A and B Inlet Valves to 45%.
  • Enable Condensate Pump 2.
  • Enable Feedwater Pump 2.
  • Open Deaerator A-B Bleed Valve.

Stage Three Startup

  • Set Main Circulation Pumps A1 and A2 to speed 3.
  • Enable Main Circulation Pump B1 and B2.
  • Set Condenser Coolant Pumps A1 and A2 to speed 3.
  • Type the numbers 3000 into the Auto Control Rod panel and click the grey button.
  • Withdraw Control rods until power reaches 3000 MW, Keep an eye on Core Heat Distribution.
  • Enable Condenser Coolant Pumps B1 and B2 and set to speed 3.

Turbine B Run-up

  • Call Engineering (the leftmost phone, dial 106), click Turning gear, and disengage Turbine B.
  • At the Turbine Panel enable the Synchroscope for Turbine B, it is on the wall
  • Turn on the auto bypass and set anywhere from 20-50MW.
  • Slowly increase the Turbine Valve to around 60-70%
  • The RPM should start rising, if it doesnt or it rises slowly you are not making enough steam; check your reactor MW.
  • Open the Grid Sync cap.
  • Once the RPM reaches around 1770-1830 RPM the Synchroscope will flash and make an audible sound when the needle passes over the zone at the top of the gauge.
  • Once the needle slows down and the RPM is around 1800, wait for the green light to light up and then click Grid Sync.
  • If you synced it correctly the RPM will stabilize at 1800.

If sync is unsuccessful


  • Click the Acknowledge button, the corresponding one to silence the Turbine Trip Alarm will be glowing.
  • Wait for the RPM to drop below 600.
  • Click the reset button next to the Acknowledge button you just pressed.
  • Retry the Turbine Sync Process.

If sync is successful


  • Acknowledge the Reverse Power alert if you have it.
  • Increase the Turbine Valve SLOWLY to 100%, use the precice valve.
  • Decrease the Bypass valve to 0% or near 0%.
  • Once the generator load is positive click the Reset button next to the Acknowledge button.
  • Call Engineering (the leftmost phone, dial 106), click reset breakers.
  • Enable Feedwater Pump 2 to speed 2.
  • Enable Condensate Pump 2 to speed 2.

Stage Four Startup

  • Type into the Auto Control Rods keypad 4000 MW.
  • Set Main Circulation Pumps B1 and B2 to speed 3.
  • Enable Condenser Coolant Pumps A3 and B3.
  • Call the Grid Controller and click Go Online.
  • Enables Bypass valves A and B.
  • Enable Auto Bypass Valves A and B.

Reactor Variability

Unfortunately due to the nature of this game there is no "demand to reactor power" equasion

Low Power Range [4000MW - 4200MW]

  • Enable Main Circulation Pumps C1 and C2.

Medium Power Range [4200MW - 4500MW]

  • Enable Main Circulation Pumps C1 and C2 and set to speed 2.
  • Set Condenser Coolant Pumps A3 and B3 to speed 2.
  • Set Condenser 1 or Feedwater 1 to speed 3

High Power Range [4500MW - 4800MW]

  • Enable Main Circulation Pumps C1 and C2 and set to speed 3.
  • Set Condenser Coolant Pumps A3 and B3 to speed 3.
